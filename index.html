Patterns & Insights for Coach KSD
The sleep plan from the Jul 9 call is working, and this is the first stretch of the program that proves it. Four consecutive nights have started before 1am: 12:34am, 12:46am, 12:45am and 12:40am. Nothing in the program had ever strung together more than one. They produced 9.5h (score 95), 7.5h (83), 7.7h (89) and 7.8h (88), so the rolling 7-night average is up to 7.4h against the 7.5h target, from 7.2h a week ago, and that average is still carrying Jul 7's 4.5h disaster night. Once that night rolls out of the window the average clears target on its own. Mean onset improved to 1:39am, mean wake 9:52am.
Onset is the whole game, and the evidence is now overwhelming. Every one of the six earliest onsets of the program sits in its top tier of scores, and the four most recent (all sub-1am) scored 95, 83, 89 and 88. Against that, the 4:10am start on Jul 7 gave 4.5h at score 65, the worst night in the window, and Jul 8's 2:10am start needed a 12:45pm lie-in to bank 8.4h. Bedtime, not wake time, not discipline in the morning, is the single variable that moves this page. Protect the three levers from the call: cleaner 2-3x/week, fixed bedtime routine, dates done by 8pm.
The wake anchor has locked. The last three mornings came in at 9:01am, 9:08am and 9:08am, all within eight minutes of the 9:00 target, and they did it without an alarm-shaped crash on the other end because the onsets were early. Mean wake still reads 9:52am, but that number is being dragged entirely by two nights now behind us: Jul 8's 12:45pm recovery lie-in and Jul 10's 10:52am. Onset early plus wake at 9 is the combination the whole program was built to produce, and it has now happened three nights running.
Recovery is solid on the surface, but HRV is quietly sliding and it deserves a look. The window averages read readiness 79 (range 61-86), HRV 51ms and resting HR 50bpm, all improved on last week. Resting HR is genuinely good, hitting 47 twice in the last three nights. But HRV has fallen four nights straight: 57, 56, 51, 46, and this morning's 46 is the lowest reading since the 4.5h night, despite 7.8h of sleep and a score of 88. Oura's own recovery-index contributor dropped to 19 this morning. Sleep is not the cause here, so the suspects are elsewhere: Muay Thai load starting Jul 7, the under-eating pattern Krista flagged, or alcohol. Worth watching for two more nights before reading anything into it, but do not let a good sleep score hide it.
Deep sleep is the one metric the good nights have not fixed. Averages sit at deep 83 min against the 90-min target and REM 113 min (comfortably over). Jul 10's monster night banked 127 min of deep, but the three good nights since managed only 58, 89 and 80 min, so deep is not simply tracking total sleep the way it did earlier in the program. It tends to track the front half of the night, which is exactly what alcohol and late eating compress. If the food journal (Priority 2) starts capturing dinner timing, this is the metric to correlate it against.
Five sessions in the last fourteen days, but three straight rest days now.
