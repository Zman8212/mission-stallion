The three things that matter

1. Life-maintenance is eating my life

62% of my waking hours go to the lower tier. Life-maintenance alone (cleaning, laundry, errands, getting ready, eating) is 29 hours, which is bigger than money, meditation, and fitness combined (22.4h). The biggest slice is actually getting ready and grooming, about 12h, not chores. Cleaning and laundry together are only about 5.4h, but they land late at night, which is what pushes bedtime to 2:30am. That makes this not just a time problem, but the cause of the sleep problem below.

2. Sleep is a bedtime problem, not a sleep problem

Sleep onsets this week: 2:00, 12:15, 2:04, 1:17, 2:30, 2:30. Six nights, five of them past 1am. The exception proves it: the one night I was in bed by 12:15 scored 92, my best of the week by 10 points. I fall asleep fine once I am down. I just do not get down, because chores and late work push bedtime to 2:30.

3. Money time is real but lopsided

Money time was 11.2h, but zero hours Saturday and Sunday, and it is all weekday. Tuesday's 4.8h, my biggest day, happened entirely after 6pm, chopped into three blocks by laundry. I can do the work. It just gets squeezed into late-night fragments after the day's maintenance eats the daylight.

The three highest-leverage changes

1
Outsource the chores. A cleaner plus laundry handled for me. This is the master lever: it buys back about 10 hours a week and removes the thing pushing bedtime to 2:30am.
2
Hard bedtime anchor. The data proves in-bed-by-midnight equals a 92 night. Pick a lights-out time and let whatever does not fit die or get outsourced.
3
Protect one daylight money block. Money time keeps getting shoved to post-6pm fragments. Put one 2 to 3 hour block in the morning or early afternoon, before maintenance floods the day.
